Changing your oil

Post By : Auto Repair February 12, 2023

Changing your car's oil is one of the most important things you can do to keep it running smoothly

It's also one of the easiest things to do yourself, and it can save you a lot of money in the long run.
Here's a step-by-step guide on how to change your car's oil without needing a mechanic:

  • Gather your supplies. You'll need a new oil filter, new oil, a drain pan, a wrench or socket, and a funnel. You may also want to wear gloves and safety glasses to protect yourself from the oil.

  • Locate the oil drain plug The oil drain plug is usually located on the bottom of the engine. It's a large bolt with a washer on it

  • Place the drain pan under the drain plug Be careful not to spill any oil.

  • Use the wrench or socket to loosen the drain plug Once the plug is loose, remove it by hand and let the oil drain into the pan.

  • Replace the drain plug Once the oil has drained, tighten the drain plug with the wrench or socket
  • Locate the oil filter The oil filter is usually located on top of the engine. It's a large metal canister with a rubber gasket on the bottom

  • Use the wrench or socket to loosen the oil filter Once the filter is loose, remove it by hand

  • Apply a thin coat of new oil to the gasket of the new oil filter This will help create a seal and prevent leaks

  • Screw on the new oil filter by hand Don't over-tighten it, or you could damage the filter

  • Add new oil to the engine The amount of oil you need will depend on your car's make and model. Check your owner's manual for the correct amount

  • Replace the oil cap remove any air bubbles


  • Start the engine and let it run for a few minutes This will circulate the new oil and help

  • Check the dipstick The dipstick is usually located on the side of the engine. Pull it out and wipe it clean with a rag. Then, insert it back into the engine and check the oil level. The oil level should be between the "full" and "add" marks

  • Dispose of the old oil and filter properly You can take them to a local auto parts store or recycling center



That's it! You've now successfully changed your car's oil without needing a mechanic. Congratulations!
